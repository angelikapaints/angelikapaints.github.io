“Ich bin eine Berlinerin!”

Angelika was born and raised in the vibrant city Berlin Germany. She recalls her most significant artistic influences from growing up within a multi-cultural environment, visiting art galleries, museums, theatre, and music venues from an early childhood. Throughout her childhood and young adult life, she was exposed and involved in art making and learned from an early age to express herself visually. Angelika’s first medium was photography with a home-made darkroom in her parent’s home, she developed her own film and enlarged her own black and prints. She later moved to painting and acquired a large home-build easel that took up most of her room to the dismay of her parents and the carpet in her room. Nevertheless, her parents were encouraging Angelika her artistic growth in her teenage years up to today. When Angelika met her husband who was an US Army Officer, she was drawn to move into a new country and study abroad. She moved to the US with her husband living in Arizona, California, Virginia, Maryland, and back to Europe in The Netherlands and Germany. Angelika’s prerogatives in raising her children has been to raise them bilingually, engrossing them in art making as well as teaching them about cultural acceptance through art, and the love for life-long learning.

Public and Personal Art in One Piece
The public experience from my art is when the art is seen from further away. It might just be a glimpse from a corner of your eye without seeing the details. It sets a mood incorporating a specific color palette. It’s like picking up an aroma of a new or familiar food that is simmering in the kitchen. It’s the foretaste that becomes more clear and only gives away its nuances when fully enjoyed and experienced through all senses.

Embracing life with the mantra that there is always an opportunity to learn. Her favourite years were when she was herself a student studying art at UMBC and again for her Graduate degree at MICA (Maryland Institute College of Art). Her formative years offered a plethora of immersive experiences; learning from different cultures, getting involved in new environments and communities, and never ceasing to be amazed by novel  personal and professional challenges. Angelika has been living, creating art, and teaching to a broad community of learners with diverse backgrounds, cultures, and learning profiles. Living in the USA for almost 20 years, she visited Europe at least once a year collecting new ideas for her classroom and reconnecting with her personal roots.

Teaching at an International School, using both her artistic experience and philosophy of life-long learning to teach a variety of learners with different skill levels and learning abilities. Today, Angelika teaches art and STEAM at the International School in Neu-Ulm, Germany getting inspired by runs along the Donau and getting acquainted with the German life again.
