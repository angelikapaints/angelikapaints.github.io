“Ich bin eine Berlinerin!”

Born and raised in the vibrant city Berlin Germany. Some of my most significant artistic influences from growing up within a multi-cultural environment, visiting art galleries, museums, theatre, and music venues from an early childhood. Throughout my childhood and young adult life, I was exposed and involved in art making and learned from an early age to express myself visually. My parents always encouraged me to do what fullfills me in order to be happy in life. My dad once told me: "what's the use if you have a profession that makes you miserable because then you make everyone else around you miserable too!" Creating art is exactly that for me: It fullfills me all the way from my head to my toes ;-)

My first medium was photography with a home-made darkroom in my parent’s home, I developed my own film and enlarged my own black and prints all in the bathroom. Later, I moved to painting and acquired a large home-build easel. When I met my (ex)husband who was an US Army Officer, I was drawn to move into a new country and study abroad. I moved to the US living in Arizona, California, Virginia, Maryland, and back to Europe in The Netherlands and Germany. I had been my prerogatives to raise my three children bilingually, engrossing them in art making as well as teaching them about cultural acceptance through art, and the love for life-long learning.

Art Education & Art Educator
Embracing life with the mantra that there is always an opportunity to learn. My favourite years were when I was studying art at UMBC and again for as a graduate student at MICA (Maryland Institute College of Art). My formative years offered a plethora of immersive experiences; learning from different cultures, getting involved in new environments and communities, and never ceasing to be amazed by novel  personal and professional challenges. I have been living, creating art, and teaching to a broad community of learners with diverse backgrounds, cultures, and learning profiles. Living in the USA for almost 20 years, I visited Europe at least once a year collecting new ideas for my classroom and reconnecting with my personal roots.

Since 2020, I have been teaching teaching art and STEAM at the International School in Ulm/Neu-Ulm, Germany getting inspired by runs along the Donau and acquainted with the beautiful German life again, and spending more time with family and friends.
